Welcome to Bhubaneswer

  • Historical Significance: Bhubaneswar is often referred to as the "Temple City of India" due to its numerous ancient temples and historical significance. The city has a rich heritage of Hindu architecture, with many temples dating back to the 7th century. The Lingaraja Temple, dedicated to Lord Shiva, is one of the most prominent and revered temples in the city.

  • Cultural Heritage:The city is a hub of art, culture, and heritage. It hosts various classical dance forms like Odissi, which originated in the state of Odisha. The Odisha State Museum and several cultural festivals and fairs celebrate the region's vibrant traditions.

  • Modern Development: Bhubaneswar has seen significant modernization in recent years and has emerged as a prominent economic and educational center in eastern India. The city has a well-planned infrastructure with wide roads, parks, and a modern airport.

  • Greenery and Parks: Bhubaneswar is known for its green spaces and parks, earning it the nickname "Green City." The Nandankanan Zoological Park, located on the outskirts of the city, is a famous wildlife conservation center.
